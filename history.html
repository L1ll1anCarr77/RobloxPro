History and Development

The beta version of Roblox was created by co-founders David Baszucki and Erik Cassel in 2004, which was named DynaBlocks. Baszucki tested the demos that year. In 2005, the company changed the name to Roblox and was launched on September 1, 2006. In March 2007, Roblox used COPPA(Children's Online Privacy Protection Rule) which restricted communication ability of users under the age of 13 years by restricting them to selecting predefined messages from a menu. In August, Roblox applied server improvements and released a premium membership service called "Builders Club"

In December 2011, Roblox held it's first Hack Week, which is an annual event where Roblox developers work on outside the box ideas for new developments to present. On December 11, 2012, an iOS version of Roblox was released, and on July 16, 2014 an Android version was released. On October 1, 2013, Roblox released its Developer Exchanges program, which allows developers to exchange Robux earned from their games into real world currencies.

On May 31, 2015, a feature called Smooth Terrain was added, increasing the graphical fidelity of the terrain and changing the physics engine from a block oriented style to a smoother and more realistic one.

In April 2016, Roblox launched Roblox VR for the Oculus Rift. At the time of release, more than ten million games were available in 3D. AT around the same time, the safe chat feature was removed and it was replaced by a system based on whitelist, which has a set of acceptable words for users under the age of 13 years old and a set of blacklisted words for other users.

Throughout 2017, Roblox had many updates to its server technology, as the technology they were using was out of date and lead to outages. Also in 2017, Roblox closed it's official forums. In 2018, players could no longer play as a guest. In July 2020, Roblox announced the creation of "Party Place", which is an online hangout.

In December 2021, Roblox shut down its Chinese servers, which they claimed that they were attempting to create "Another Version" of the app that allowed Chinese players to access the platform. In July 2022, an archives of internal documents related to the platform's activities in China were leaked by an anonymous hacker.

In December 2022, Roblox corporation announced that it planned to add an agee rating system, which allowed for moderate portrayals of violence in games flagged to be suitable for players 13 and over.

On July 2023, Roblox was released as a public beta for the Meta Quest 2 and Meta Quest Pro. The beta was downloaded over one million times within five days.

On September 2023, it was announced that Roblox would be made available on the PlayStation thta following month, with the Meta Quest versions being made publicly available later in September.
